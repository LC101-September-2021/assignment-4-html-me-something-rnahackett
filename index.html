Sustain

Why sustainability?

We are often excited by new discoveries and innovations, yet we also need to sustain what we know works. How we can ensure that effective public health programs continue to provide benefits to our communities? Money may be one of the first things to come to mind, but a sustainable program often has more than funding. Looking across programs, you often see that, even with the same level of funding, some programs are able to sustain themselves and others are not. The Program Sustainability Framework identifies eight factors that can help programs withstand challenges and changes. This may look like adapting your services to a crisis like a pandemic. Or it may be more routine such as planning how to continue your services once a grant ends. We define program sustainability as the ability to maintain programming and its benefits over time.

Action planning process

Sustaining programs takes active planning. The Program Sustainabiilty Action PLanning process involves three steps programs can take to build capacity for sustainability by strengthening one or more of the eight sustainability domains. The three steps are:

1.Understand

The first step is to build your understanding of the factors that impact your program sustainability.

2.Assess

3.Plan
